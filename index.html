Intro

My love for coding was definitely something that was gradual in my lifetime. It all started with hardware and building computers at a young age, my first tower at the age of 10. I remember having a couple pre-built brand computers in the house and taking them apart just to put them back together. PC gaming online was also a huge part of my early years and naturally, I became more interested in all things related to the web. My curiosity in building things from scratch transgressed from hardware into software. I taught myself a lot of basics in CSS/HTML/& JavaScript at some point in high school, crafting very simple web sites and learning the fundamentals to web design.

Most of that came to a halt when college came around. Decisions were made that pushed me down a different road. A long story short, I opted to follow into healthcare & emergency services. Fast forward a few years after working for private companies & still waiting to get onto a fire department, I truly felt unfulfilled, but only in the sense that I wasn't getting to utilize my creativity; it plagued me. I heard about all these coding bootcamps and really thought about a lifestyle change. After about of year of contemplation, I decided that it was time to chase the new challenge and possibly re-ignite an old fire. Technology was always the field that interested me the most and that was my strongest motivator in my decision to switch career paths. I started Devbootcamp Chicago in October 2016 and graduated in March 2017 & I can truly say that it was the best decision of my life. Everything I learned from the hard skills to the soft skills, it gave me a better understanding of how to truly be a contributor and collaborator in a software development setting. Looking towards the future, I feel nothing but excitement as my desire to learn is matched by the progression of technology.

Squad

Ruby on Rails / JavaScript / Twilio API / AWS S3 / Travis CI / React Native

Squad was our capstone project for Dev Bootcamp, built in 8 days by a team of 4. Our idea was to build a mobile friendly application that allows a user to create a friends list and send out polls/questions when they're out and about, needing quick advice. The user is then able to watch the feedback come back in real time via chart update.

Round Table

Ruby on Rails / JavaScript / Travis CI

Round table was a mock client project, built in 2 days by a team of 4 where we practiced client interaction from a tech team perspective. We were tasked to build out an application to the client's specifications, with input from our team. The app itself is very similar to a wikipedia with its features. Adhering to proposed deadlines (MVP showcase) with the client changing requirements were also simulated.
